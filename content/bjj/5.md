I didn't feel as if I learnt much in the training section of the class, it was
a little more technical compared to what I have done previously and I feel like
as soon as the instructor was finished explaining the move, I completly forgot
it when putting it in practice.

I did learn however that when you are on mount, and you want to restrict arm
movement, shuffle your legs forward towards the armpits and you'll get a nice
position where you can then start looking for a sub.

During our rolls in class, I have been put into what I know is called a closed
guard. For me this is very difficult to get out of, so I took the time to see
some techniques to get out of this hold.

On my search I also discovered the exact training from my second or third
class. What they were teaching in that class was some side-control attack
techniques, I'm really happy I found this.

[Side-Control Attack System](https://youtu.be/jxcJMSNC1j4?si=MTO28J9TcIvnYe69)

Even though I felt a little dumb in the training segment, I feel I'm slowly
building a learning style which I think is a little more important.
